About

I am a recent mathematics graduate from MIT (2025) with research interests in low-dimensional topology and geometry. My work focuses on knot theory, Heegaard Floer homology, and discrete geometric analysis on graphs. I am currently applying to PhD programs for Fall 2025.

Email: archanam@mit.edu

Research Interests

My primary interests lie in low-dimensional topology, particularly knot theory and 3-manifold invariants. I am especially interested in Heegaard Floer homology and its applications to detecting geometric structures. I also have experience in discrete geometric analysis, where I have studied ancient solutions to heat equations on graphs.

Selected Research Experience

Visiting Student Researcher - Imperial College London (2025)

Working with Professor Steven Sivek on using pseudo-Anosov maps and branched coverings to investigate whether Heegaard Floer homology detects the genus 3 torus knot T(2,5).

Undergraduate Research - MIT Mathematics (2023)

Collaborated with Tang-Kai Lee to establish sharp bounds on spaces of ancient solutions to discrete heat equations on weighted graphs, resulting in a publication in the Proceedings of the AMS.

Proceedings of the American Mathematical Society, 2025
